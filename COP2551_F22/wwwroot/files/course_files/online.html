"Web" Distance-learning

By now, most of us have experienced online courses and you're probably wondering how this one is going to work.

Like most of the courses you've probably experienced at this school, our course material will be contained here within the Canvas online system, and accessed through the school's website.

Important reminders:

  • remote, or distance-learning does not mean independent learning, and
  • online does not mean self-paced

 

"We are not alone."

This course is what's known as an asynchronous course. That means that we will not have scheduled meetings, either online or face-to-face. people take online courses for a variety of reasons. Traditionally, the primary factor was schedule flexibility, to work around work or family schedules. More recently of course a global health crisis changed the way people live and work. Working and going to school remotely didn't begin with the pandemic, but it certainly became more common and more accepted.

 

There are benefits to being online from whatever location works best for you, but there are also down-sides. When studying a technical subject, there may be times of frustration when things just aren't making sense, and in spite of what I'm telling you, you'll look around your workspace and feel very alone. You are not. There is a Canvas discussion board to ask (and answer!) questions to and from other students.

The format of my online office hours is intended to be for open technical discussion. Because of this, I generally separate meeting times for different classes, although I may be available at other times as well. Check the syllabus for the times arranged for this class, and send me an email if you'd like to request another time, or have a question that is more appropriate for a one-on-one discussion.

All times listed are local in Gainesville, FL. It is my sincere hope that these times will fit with your schedule.

Office hours are hosted in Zoom. These are completely optional, and not planned, or structured lessons or lectures. These are open sessions and students are invited to connect and bring your questions and issues with the projects that you have. Students will guide the discussion and I will be available to provide answers and explanations. Students are encouraged to answer each other's questions also. Be aware, due to the open nature of these meetings, if you have a question of a more individual or personal nature, I will work with you to schedule an alternate meeting time.

Please note, these are scheduled one hour blocks, but as long as I have no schedule conflict, these discussions may frequently go on longer. On the other hand, on those occasions that noone connects to the meeting, I may close the meeting after waiting for 20-30 minutes.

 

Not self-paced

The school has scheduled a block of time and resources for each class to complete a set of learning objectives. It should be no surprise that there are many factors considered in that scheduling. Likewise, each course is divided into smaller pieces of achievable blocks of learning and, like in our case, these build on each other.

By keeping up with the schedule as designed, there is time to complete the assignments, and I have time to grade them and get feedback to you for next time.
