Sequential Logic

A sequence is a set of events or things that come one after another in a particular order.

So much of what we do every day requires decisions and sequential logic

  • Open the door before you walk through it
  • If it is cold outside, grab a coat
  • While there is still work to do, keep workng
  • For all four car tires, check the air pressure

Another example

Park your car > turn off the engine > remove the key > take off your seatbelt > gather your things > open the car door > get out of the car > close the car door > IF the car is outside, THEN lock the car > walk to the front door > IF the front door is locked AND you have a key, unlock the door, ELSE knock on the door and wait for someone to open it > walk inside > IF it is dark, THEN turn on the lights.

Of course this is a lot of detail that you don't need to think about all the time, maybe "go home" is enough to be understood.

 

When you stop and consider, you will realize that everything you do all day is a series of things, and many of them are intentionally ordered in a logical sequence.

Maybe you have driven to an appointment, walked from your car and across a parking lot and up a set of stairs and remembered that you forgot something in your car. The logic demands that you consider this new data and make a decision. You can go to the appointment without the thing, or walk back to the car and get it and come back, or walk back to the car and make another decision, maybe the walk was too far, and either move your car closer, or call and reschedule the appointment for another day.

Pseudocode

Pseudo is an adjective which means false or immitation.

A farmer might take an old set of worn out work clothes, stuff them with straw, add a hat and stand it up in a field. A scarecrow is a pseudo-farmer, or an imitation person intended to frighten away birds to keep them from eating the farmers crops. It cannot do the work of a farmer, it is not intended to fool anyone into thinking it is anything more than what it is, but for the purpose of scaring crows, it is adequate and more efficient than hiring an extra farm-hand to stand next to the fence all day scaring away the birds.

Pseudocode is the simple expression of a logical sequence of steps that a process will take to complete a task. It is not intended to fool anyone into thinking that it is actual code. It is not meant to be executable by a computer it is simply the logical steps expressed in simple terms.

 

For example, you might read the statement: 

if(x>y){y++}

if open parenthesis letter 'x' pointing to letter 'y', close parenthesis, and then add letter 'y' two times.

In most C-style languages (C/C++/C#/Java), this is a logical and acceptable statement. Some of you might already know that it really means:

"if the value of x is greater than the value of y, then add 1 to the value of y."

By now you know that the first is sourcecode the second is pseudocode.

Pseudocode should contain clear, simple terms. Imagine you were sharing it with your grandmother or the random person on the street. If they try to read it and have questions about what it means, it might not be clear enough.
