Graph Structures

A graph data structure is a collection of nodes or vertices connected by edges, representing relationships or connections between them. It is used to model and analyze complex relationships and dependencies in various domains, such as computer science, social networks, transportation systems, and more.

In this module, you will:

  • Investigate non-linear graph data structures, consisting of entities (nodes) and relations (vertices).
