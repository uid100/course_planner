So what can you expect?

There are 15 weeks in the term. We are using one of Tony Gaddis' stepping through... series of books, for the C++ language. Take a day or two now to refresh your knowledge of C++ or to pick up the syntax if it isn't familiar. We'll be starting in Chapter 2, but quickly jumping to more advanced concepts. You'll be using this book, and probably need to refer to others, and online for more background information, and trying out the concepts to explore more.

 

We will use the Canvas course as a guide through the subject material. This course is not new, and not new to me, but the presentation in Canvas is getting a complete refresh this semester, and for that reason, you will notice that although the modules are present, there is a lot of content in later modules that is not available to you yet. You may also notice that each module may have a note on it indicating the content will unlock on a particular date. As the content is refreshed and reviewed, I will be opening that up to you, in most cases in advance of those dates. I am teaching multiple classes at multiple schools this semester. This is a priority, but just know that I want to ensure the material is ready for you before I release it. Also, please let me know if you see something that doesn't make sense, or doesn't look correct, and I'll do my best to clarify, or correct the information as quickly as I can. Thanks in advance for your patience and help!

 

Speaking of online, the college lists this as a web course. You'll also hear it referred to as asynchronous. We won't have scheduled meetings, but we do have a class schedule to accomplish. The Canvas course is designed to guide you through, but we'll do it together, and I will make an effort to not let you feel alone through the process.
