My Web App: User Interface

UI/UX

In your studies, you have seen and followed different patterns and frameworks and models. Programming logic often begins with pseudocode, unconcerned with syntax and coding standards, this method is a freeform discussion to walk through processing steps.

Flowcharts became a standard in manufacturing during the industrial revolution and have undergone many standards revisions.

Commonly in software engineering, Unified Modeling Language (UML) diagrams are used to communicate structural and behavioral dimensions of an application. Selections among these design tools vary from situation to situation, and organization to organization.

As a part of the design process, peer review is often included even for these early artifacts and it may come as a surprise how often logic and data errors and issues can be corrected early, well before solution implementation.

For graphical applications, wireframe drawings are often used to help plan, communicate, and visualize not only the user interface, but also the data logic. Often, a whiteboard sketch of screen (view) after screen after screen, may reveal an oversight in which data element is needed from one processing step to another or identify which data properties need to be populated and validated at which phase of the process. It can even help steer architecture and component structure.

Wow, that is a lot!

It is. Sometimes, ideas may start out as sketches on a restaurant napkin and be refined on a whiteboard in a conversation with stakeholders. The customer may not know the ideal solution until they see the wrong one(s). The engineering team may be aware of all the latest technology, but not have a clear vision of the user's environment.

 

Objective

The main purpose of the wireframe drawing is to communicate an idea, and to confirm that the design is logical. It needs to make sense and be simple to the user, it also needs to ensure that the data required to render each view is available when it is needed.

Design the User Interface for your web application.

 

Instructions

Wireframes

As part of your ongoing web project development, you will develop two or three page designs for your application. These can be done in html(and css) code or may be sketches. My preferred drawing tool for this is the sketchy template in the web-based draw.io tool.

Whichever tool you select to create your graphical concept, be sure to save the drawings as image file(s).

 

Deliverable
