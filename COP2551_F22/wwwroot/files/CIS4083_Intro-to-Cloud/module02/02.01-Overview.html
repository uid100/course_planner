Cloud Service Providers

Why?

In the last module, we talked about what the cloud is an about the concept of outsourcing common services to third parties.

If you needed to fly from New York City to London, you probably would not start by building a plane and hiring someone to fly it, instead you could identify one of several passenger air carriers which provide that service and buy a ticket to fly on one of their airplanes.

If you then needed to go on to Paris or Frankfurt, you could simply buy another ticket and fly there.

To extend the metaphor, if you needed to fly your entire team to another city, you would have the option of chartering an entire plane to take them on that flight. But you only pay for the flights that you need, and you rely on the flight crew to fly the plane and the ground crew to keep up with maintenance and fuel and other operations of airplane and airline management.

Likewise, if you need more computing power, you can simply configure your cloud services to deliver more capability for the time that you need it and turn it off when the project ends.

In this course, we will leverage resources from the industry and dive into real-world situations and apply real-world solutions. The intent is to form a good understanding of the cloud in general terms, as an architecture and business model and systems solution. In the course of exploring hands-on examples, you will see differences in the way things are accomplished from one provider to the next and even how they are named, but try to identify the similarities in the services and the architectures.
