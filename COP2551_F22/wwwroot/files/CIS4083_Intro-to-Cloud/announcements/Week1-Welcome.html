I'm excited about this course. The topic and the technology is emerging. My intent is to keep the information fresh. Together, we'll have some extra work to do.

Often "introduction to--" courses consist of reading through a thoughtfully selected textbook, designed to describe a topic and perhaps invite the reader to try some simplified examples to apply the principles as their presented. This is a typical and appropriate approach to learning a new skill or gathering information about a subject.

In this course, we have made the conscious decision to take another approach. There will be outside content to read, or view and consider, but more importantly, we will consider the cloud and cloud technologies from an engineering and business impact from a much more hands-on perspective.

The tools we will employ and explore are literally online, so that is where we will go for information about them.

Speaking of online, the college lists this as a web course. You'll also hear it referred to as asynchronous. We won't have scheduled meetings, but we do have a class schedule to accomplish. The Canvas course is designed to guide you through it, but we'll do it together, and I will make an effort to not let you feel alone through the process.
