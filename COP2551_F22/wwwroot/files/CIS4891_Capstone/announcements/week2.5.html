I really enjoyed your project ideas, and while you will be asked to build one of them out step-by-step as we go through the semester, but that doesn't mean you need to limit your own development projects. Just as I have different parallel projects in my garage, I also have several works-in-progress on GitHub. Having multiple projects is perfectly acceptable. And if you prefer, you can close the garage door by making the others private repositories. I have taught this curriculum on previous occasions and although it isn't too often, it's also not uncommon for a student to get to a point and get frustrated or shift their interest to another project. Of course the later in the semester we progress, the more difficult that is. But if you have parallel projects that you are working, maybe because you are on the fence making a decision or just have some extra time, having the extra projects might just come in handy.

It's of course not a requirement or expectation to be working on multiple projects, but it is an option.
