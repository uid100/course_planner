The design documents in your readme will tell a visitor a story and communicate your vision for your project application.

Your project repository is a work-in-progress. This week, you'll be adding to it. It's a good idea and usually a project requirement, to share project progress and status with others to get feedback and comments.

In a formal project, there are a series of phased reviews after a development contract is in place. Commonly, the requirements will go through a round of reviews, culminating with a final requirements review, which is formally agreed to and signed off by the customer, before the design phase officially kicks off.

At some point, the design team presents their preliminary design documents to the customer and other stakeholders to get approval that the design is consistent with the requirements and is achievable and that it all are in agreement. Like other reviews, this review activity may repeat some number of times until the stakeholders agree on the project direction.

Sometimes the customer doesn't know what they want until they see what they don't want.
