Requirements

Many of the concepts we will introduce and discuss will be generalizations and simplified examples of much larger topics. This is true with requirements management and testing. In this module we will introduce the concepts.

Requirements management begins with identifying the customer needs, and through stakeholder discussion, evolves into verifiable measures of completion.

Testing

Test activities begin at the start of a project. Unsurprisingly, successful test management is closely associated with requirements management.

Testing includes test planning, process definition and monitoring, and verification and validation (V&V).

Design Review

Formal project management is built around a series of milestones and decision points. Periodically, throughout a project's life cycle, internal and external stakeholders will conduct formal and informal reviews to ensure a project is proceeding as expected, to make corrections when warranted, and to guide project decisions.
