Module 9

  • Delivering the Product

 

Each project is different, and contains elements of planning, design, implementation, test, delivery, maintenance and support.

Processes need to be tailored between projects because every project is unique and has its own set of requirements, objectives, constraints, risks, and stakeholders.

Even though you will customize and adjust the processes and procedures to fit the specific needs and characteristics of each project, it's important to remember that the processes associated with each phase may be tailored, but should not be disregarded.

Tailoring the processes involves assessing the project's size, complexity, scope, schedule, budget, and other relevant factors, and then determining which project management processes and practices should be used, modified, or excluded to meet the project's goals and deliverables. This helps to ensure that the project is managed efficiently, effectively, and in a way that maximizes the chances of success.

    At the end of this module, you will be able to:
  • Describe the activities associated with a Test-Readiness Review (TRR)
  • Explain the practical use of a Requirements Traceability Matrix (RTM)
