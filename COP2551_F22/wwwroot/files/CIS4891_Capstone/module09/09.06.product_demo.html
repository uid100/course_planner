Product Demo

This course is about understanding and following processes. There are assumptions of some prior knowledge of programming and building applications, but the focus has really been the design artifacts and the development process.

We are hopeful that you have built something that you are proud to have your name on, but you will notice that there has not been much (if any) emphasis on code review or quantitative test results. I am interested in seeing what you have built, but I am more interested in the development of the product.

Everyone hopes to have a market-ready app that is going to disrupt the industry with life-altering content. That is not the expectation. Recall that an objective at the end of each sprint using scrum methodology is a potentially shippable product. Shippable does not always mean market-ready sometimes it means something is ready and maybe preliminary testing can take place. Or maybe it doesn't meet all of the requirements (yet), but it's enough to show the customer so that they can see product direction and progress.

Even complete products which meet all requirements and are fully tested often still have a backlog of proposed or planned features that are not included in a particular release.

Objective

Then what is the point?

    Prepare a presentation of at least 5 but no more than 7 minutes to demonstrate the end state of your project, and progress made toward that goal. Use a stopwatch, there will be points off for under or over the time requirements.
  • The presentation may be using PointPoint slides with slide narration on each slide. Insructions for doing this are easy to find online. A benefit of this approach is that each slide can be separately recorded and pieced together.
    After creating your presentation, select File > Export > Create a video. Select the lowest quality that maintains the detail you need to keep the file smaller.
  • Another alternative is to record this on your computer. There are many tools available for doing this. Zoom is available to you and is relatively easy one to use for making videos which incude running demonstrations on your computer.
  • There are other tools available also. You may investigate and select from others.

 

    Your presentation should describe:
  1. code and architecture walk-through
  2. the project experience including each phase of the SDLC and the project artifacts (design documents, requirements trace, test reports...)
  3. product demonstration
