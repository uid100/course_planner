Final Design Review

At this point, you have developed a number of typical design artifacts in your GitHub repository. These have all been arranged in your project README.md file.

I asked you to add a header with each of the sections you added. Take a few minutes now to add a table of contents near the top of the document. With defined headers in your markdown document it is really quite straightforward to do, and it will help you see your project's organization at a glance. Help doing that can be found here.

The design documents in your readme will tell a visitor a story and communicate your vision for your project application.

It may seem confusing. For several weeks, I've asked you to take the time to develop design documents and artifacts, tables of requirements and UML drawings. Then in this module, I introduced the ideas that as an industry, we need to become more agile with more focus on product and less on process and documentation.

But really these are not mutually exclusive. In some of those projects I mentioned, we kept a team of 5 or 6 engineers full-time employed working on requirements management and design documentation for years at a time. I've delivered Detailed Design Documentation (DDD) and System Requirements Specification (SRS) documents that were many hundreds of pages long adhering to strict formal standards. These are not bad things and they are not going away.

What I am suggesting, as the scrum video you just saw indicated, we still need to create the design and the requirements, but rather than assume an end-to-end vision, we need

  • just enough planning to start
  • and just enough design to begin implementation
  • and just enough requirements to develop our test cases.

It's still good to have a plan, but we need to plan to get started quickly.
