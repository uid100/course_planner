Get Ready and Go!

This week you'll read through Chapter 2. You will want to follow these examples as you progress through the chapter. You should be trying out each step through the book. Yes, it will take some time. For these projects, I strongly recommend that you use the .NET3.1 (LTS) build target. There were major changes to the C# language in versions 9 and 10, released with .NET 5 and .NET 6. These changes are most noticeably in the visual studio project templates. If you use the .NET 6.0 target framework, the templates will be different enough that a lot of the detail in the book will be hard to follow. 

Please, use .NET 3.1 for now. The instructions in the book will make a lot more sense.

Give yourself plenty of time to work through this chapter and the exercises. This is not the week to procrastinate! There is a lot going on in this chapter, and if this is the first time that you are seeing it, It may not make sense. Follow the instructions carefully. Send your questions (or post on Discord) when you get stuck or when you have moments of enlightenment!

 

Student Web Application Development

Just to give you a little sneak peak, Next week we'll be starting work on a new project. This one is yours. The project requirements, the projects specifications, the customer, the project manager, the design and development team, the test team will consist of you.

Over the course of many weeks, we will be building useful web applications of our own designs. Most web applications are visited and viewed by mobile devices. Most of the time, with iPhone (iOS) or Android phones or tables. Your mobile application could target the device and you could build native applications, but building web applications instead makes your process available and accessible to anyone with a network connection, and virtually any device type. This is one of the reasons I find building web applications so satisfying.

For class, we will be focusing on the process more than the product. You may decide to build one project or many. In my garage, I have several projects in various stages on a number of different workbenches. All are valid and each is important for its own purpose, but each is expected to have a beginning, some intermediate steps, and (hopefully) an ending. These projects will be a yours to showcase some of the good work that you are doing, and also show your enthusiasm for the subject -maybe for potential employers, more about that later.

Next week you'll start by identifying a project or two that you want to design and build, and we'll be writing up your design plans and ideas and sharing those ideas with one another.

For this week, to get ready, start thinking about the web applications that you know and use. Get a good definition in your own mind of what really is a web application.

The fun is just getting started!
